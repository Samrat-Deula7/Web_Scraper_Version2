Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about studying in Japan

What are the basic requirements to study in Japan?

Basic requirements include: Completion of 12 years of education, basic Japanese language proficiency (varies by institution), financial capability proof, valid passport, and medical certificate. Specific requirements may vary by institution and course.

How long are the Japanese language courses?

Japanese language courses typically range from 6 months to 2 years, depending on your starting level and target proficiency. Intensive courses can prepare you for JLPT N5-N1 levels within 1-2 years of study.

Can I work part-time while studying in Japan?

Yes, international students can work part-time for up to 28 hours per week during school terms and up to 40 hours per week during holidays, with proper permission. We provide guidance on finding part-time jobs in Japan.

What is the cost of studying in Japan?

Costs vary by institution and location. Language schools typically cost ¥600,000-¥900,000 per year. University tuition ranges from ¥500,000 to ¥1,500,000 annually. Living expenses average ¥80,000-¥120,000 per month outside Tokyo.

How long does the visa process take?

The complete process typically takes 4-6 months from application to visa issuance. This includes document preparation, school application, Certificate of Eligibility processing (2-3 months), and visa application (1-2 weeks).
